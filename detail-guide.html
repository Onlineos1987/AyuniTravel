Step-by-Step Umrah Guide

Your spiritual journey explained

Ihram

Enter the state of Ihram at designated Miqat points. For men: two white unstitched cloths. For women: modest Islamic dress.

Tawaf

Perform seven circuits around the Kaaba starting from the Black Stone. Pray at Maqam Ibrahim after completion.

Sa'i

Walk seven times between Safa and Marwah hills, commemorating Hagar's search for water.

Halq/Taqsir

Men shave their heads (Halq) or trim their hair (Taqsir). Women trim a fingertip's length of hair.

Visa & Travel Process

Simplified procedures for your spiritual journey

Document Requirements

  • Passport with 6+ months validity
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Vaccination certificates
  • Confirmed hotel booking
  • Return flight ticket
  • Proof of transportation arrangements

Application Process

  1. Complete online application form
  2. Upload required documents
  3. Schedule biometric appointment
  4. Pay visa processing fees
  5. Receive e-visa via email (3-5 business days)
  6. Print visa and travel documents

Note: Processing may take longer during peak seasons :cite[5]:cite[7].

Islamic Rulings & Conditions

Essential guidelines for your pilgrimage

Rules for Men

  • Wear two unstitched white cloths for Ihram
  • Refrain from cutting hair or nails during Ihram
  • Avoid scented products and perfumes
  • Maintain proper decorum in sacred places
  • Perform Tawaf and Sa'i with proper intention

Rules for Women

  • Wear modest Islamic dress covering entire body
  • May use unscented hygiene products
  • Pray in designated women's areas
  • May perform Umrah alone (without mahram) :cite[7]
  • Maintain proper hijab throughout pilgrimage

Do's of Umrah

  • Maintain pure intention (Niyyah) for Allah
  • Be patient and kind to fellow pilgrims
  • Recite Talbiyah frequently during Ihram
  • Respect the sanctity of the holy sites
  • Make abundant dua during your journey

Don'ts of Umrah

  • Don't argue or use foul language
  • Don't harm or inconvenience others
  • Don't waste resources (water, food)
  • Don't engage in worldly commerce during rituals
  • Don't neglect prayers and remembrance of Allah
